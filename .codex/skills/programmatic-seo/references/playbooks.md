# Programmatic SEO Playbooks

Use this reference when selecting or implementing a specific pSEO pattern.

## Templates

Pattern: `[type] template`

Best when the product can provide usable templates, examples, or generators.

Required value:

- Actual downloadable, editable, or copyable template.
- Preview or example output.
- Guidance specific to the use case.
- Related templates by role, industry, format, or goal.

Risks: thin pages if only title and metadata change; low value if the template is not usable.

## Curation

Pattern: `best [category]`

Best when you can rank, filter, review, or compare options better than generic listicles.

Required value:

- Clear selection criteria.
- Fresh data, pricing, reviews, features, or availability.
- Transparent ranking methodology.
- Filters for meaningful user needs.

Risks: trust issues if rankings are affiliate-only or unsupported.

## Conversions

Pattern: `[x] to [y]`

Best for calculators, unit conversion, file conversion, finance, or data transformation tools.

Required value:

- Accurate conversion result.
- Source, timestamp, and assumptions.
- Common related conversions.
- Explanation when the conversion is not straightforward.

Risks: stale rates, incorrect formulas, or pages that do not perform the conversion immediately.

## Comparisons

Pattern: `[x] vs [y]`

Best when users are actively choosing between products, tools, categories, or approaches.

Required value:

- Balanced comparison criteria.
- Feature, pricing, use-case, and tradeoff data.
- Specific recommendation by user segment.
- First-party or clearly sourced evidence.

Risks: biased claims, outdated competitor data, legal review needs.

## Examples

Pattern: `[type] examples`

Best for design, writing, workflow, code, creative, or business inspiration.

Required value:

- Real examples with images, code, screenshots, or artifacts.
- Commentary explaining why each example works.
- Filters by industry, style, goal, or format.
- Related templates or next-step CTA.

Risks: duplicate image/text content, weak permissions, shallow galleries.

## Locations

Pattern: `[service] in [location]`

Best for local services, marketplaces, directories, and area-specific availability.

Required value:

- Location-specific availability, pricing, inventory, providers, reviews, or regulations.
- Localized copy that reflects real differences.
- Map, neighborhoods, service radius, or local FAQs where useful.
- Clear contact, booking, or quote CTA.

Risks: doorway pages when content only swaps city names; fake local presence.

## Personas

Pattern: `[product] for [audience]`

Best for products serving distinct industries, roles, company sizes, or workflows.

Required value:

- Segment-specific pain points and use cases.
- Relevant features, examples, templates, integrations, or case studies.
- CTA aligned to the segment.
- Proof from customers or data in that segment.

Risks: vague persona copy that could apply to anyone.

## Integrations

Pattern: `[product A] [product B] integration`

Best for SaaS products with real integrations or automation workflows.

Required value:

- Actual integration availability or clear workaround.
- Setup steps, supported triggers/actions, limitations, and use cases.
- Screenshots, API details, or workflow examples.
- Related integrations and alternatives.

Risks: ranking for integrations that do not exist; unsupported claims.

## Glossary

Pattern: `what is [term]`

Best for technical, industry, or product education terms.

Required value:

- Clear definition.
- Practical examples.
- Related terms and internal links.
- Product relevance without forced sales copy.

Risks: generic dictionary content with no original expertise.

## Translations

Pattern: localized content in multiple languages or markets.

Best when users search in multiple languages and the product can support those markets.

Required value:

- Human-quality localization, not raw variable translation.
- Market-specific examples, units, laws, pricing, or terminology.
- Correct hreflang and localized metadata.

Risks: duplicate or poor translation quality; unsupported market claims.

## Directory

Pattern: `[category] tools`, `[category] companies`, `[category] services`

Best when you can maintain a useful database of entities.

Required value:

- Complete entity profiles.
- Sorting, filtering, categories, and comparison data.
- Freshness signals and update process.
- Editorial or data-derived insight.

Risks: stale entries, public-data-only pages, no meaningful differentiation.

## Profiles

Pattern: `[entity name]`

Best for people, companies, products, locations, or datasets with searchable entity demand.

Required value:

- Verified facts and source attribution.
- Entity-specific metrics, history, relationships, or analysis.
- Links to related entities and categories.
- Update cadence for volatile facts.

Risks: incorrect facts, privacy concerns, duplicate profile structures without substance.
